# 🎵 Someone to Stay — Vancouver Sleep Clinic

> *A fragile, luminous reminder that being human means needing someone — and that needing someone isn't weakness*

---

## 📀 About the Song

- **Released:** 2017
- **Album:** Revival
- **Genre:** Ambient Pop, Ethereal Electronic, Dream Pop
- **In one line:** A man reaches out through his loneliness to say the simplest, hardest thing: we all need someone to stay.

---

## 🎭 Themes & Emotions

"Someone to Stay" begins in the cold. Literally — the opening lyrics describe someone alone, left out, clinging to the ruins of a broken home. But the cold isn't just weather. It's the emotional temperature of isolation, of being so alone that the world itself feels hostile and frozen. Tim Bettinson, the Australian musician behind Vancouver Sleep Clinic, wrote from a place of genuine loneliness — the kind that sits in your chest like something solid, making it hard to breathe.

But the song isn't about despair. It's about the moment just before despair, when you realise you don't have to carry everything alone. The chorus — "We all need someone to stay" — is both an admission and a truth. It's the narrator dropping his guard long enough to say: I can't do this by myself. And neither can you. None of us can.

What makes the song extraordinary is its generosity. It doesn't just ask for help. It extends it. The lyrics move between "you were alone" and "we all need" — shifting from observation to solidarity. The narrator isn't just talking about his own loneliness. He's acknowledging yours too. He's saying: whatever you're going through, the solution is the same. We need each other. That's not weakness. That's being human.

The production mirrors this emotional arc. Bettinson layers ambient electronics with acoustic warmth, and in a touch that he's called a lifelong dream, the song features a children's choir. Their voices — pure, unguarded, innocent — arrive like light entering a dark room. It's the sound of hope that hasn't been damaged yet, and it transforms the song from a confession of loneliness into something that feels almost sacred.

## 📖 Lyrics: Key Lines & What They Mean

### "You were alone, left out in the cold"

**What it means:** Someone has been abandoned or isolated — physically, emotionally, or both. "Left out in the cold" is an English idiom meaning excluded or neglected.

**Why it matters:** It starts with empathy — the narrator sees someone else's pain before addressing his own. That outward focus is what makes the song feel generous rather than self-pitying.

---

### "Clinging to the ruin of your broken home"

**What it means:** Holding onto what's left of something that's already destroyed — a family, a relationship, a sense of safety. "Broken home" refers to a family that has fallen apart.

**Why it matters:** "Clinging to ruin" is a powerful image. You know it's over, but you can't let go because the ruins are all you have left.

---

### "Too lost and hurting to carry your load"

**What it means:** The burden has become too heavy. You're too disoriented by pain to keep carrying everything by yourself.

**Why it matters:** It validates the feeling of being overwhelmed. You're not failing — the load is genuinely too much for one person. That's not a character flaw. It's reality.

---

### "We all need someone to stay"

**What it means:** Every person — not just the narrator, not just the person he's addressing, but all of us — needs someone who won't leave. Someone permanent, present, and willing to be there.

**Why it matters:** The word "stay" is everything. Not "visit." Not "check in." Stay. The deepest human need isn't to be loved dramatically — it's to be loved consistently.

---

### "We all need someone to hold"

**What it means:** Physical and emotional comfort — someone whose presence makes the pain bearable, even if they can't fix it.

**Why it matters:** "Hold" is both literal and figurative. A hug. A hand. The feeling that someone has you — that you won't fall because someone is holding on.

## 🌍 Cultural & Historical Context

Vancouver Sleep Clinic is the project of Tim Bettinson, born in 1996 in Brisbane, Australia. Despite the name suggesting a Canadian origin, Bettinson chose "Vancouver" because it evoked the atmospheric, rainy, contemplative quality he wanted his music to have. He grew up listening to his father's Fleetwood Mac and Eagles records before discovering Bon Iver and Sigur Rós as a teenager — influences that are audible in every note of his work.

"Someone to Stay" was released in 2017 as part of the album *Revival*. Bettinson told Billboard that making the song was "one of those magic moments where everything just came together and the song immediately made sense." The addition of a children's choir was a dream he'd held since he started making music, and their voices give the song a quality that feels both fragile and eternal — like a prayer that children sing before they've learned to be cynical about hope.

The song found a massive audience through Spotify playlists and has become one of the defining tracks of the ambient pop genre. It resonates particularly with listeners dealing with loneliness, depression, and the difficulty of asking for help — experiences that the song addresses with such tenderness that hearing it can feel like being held by the music itself.

## 🧑‍🎤 About the Artist

Vancouver Sleep Clinic is the solo project of Tim Bettinson, an Australian ambient pop artist born in 1996 in Brisbane. His music blends ethereal electronics with acoustic warmth and layered vocals, creating soundscapes that feel like being wrapped in a blanket on a cold night. His album *Revival* (2017) established him as one of the most emotionally affecting artists in ambient pop.

## 💬 Why This Song Is Worth Your Time

"Someone to Stay" teaches you some of the most essential English for expressing vulnerability — "left out in the cold," "carry your load," and "someone to stay" are phrases you'll hear in therapy, in friendships, and in any honest conversation about what it means to need people.
