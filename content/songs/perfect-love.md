> *A worship song that sounds like a warm embrace — a declaration that God's love is enough to cast out every fear*

- **Released:** 2020
- **Album:** Wake Up Sleeper (EP)
- **Genre:** Christian Pop, Contemporary Worship
- **In one line:** A man overwhelmed by fear and doubt discovers that perfect love — God's love — drives out every shadow.

---

## 🎭 Themes & Emotions

"Perfect Love" draws its title from one of the most quoted verses in the Bible: "Perfect love casts out fear" (1 John 4:18). Austin French takes this ancient idea and wraps it in modern pop production, creating a song that functions both as a worship anthem and as an emotional lifeline for anyone battling anxiety, self-doubt, or the feeling that they're not enough.

The song's power lies in its simplicity. It doesn't try to explain theology or argue doctrine. It simply describes what it feels like to be held by something bigger than yourself — to stop fighting your fears and let a love that's stronger than your worst moments carry you. For believers, that love is God. For anyone listening, regardless of faith, the emotional experience is universal: the relief of finally putting down the weight you've been carrying.

French grew up in small-town Georgia, dreaming of writing songs that shared his faith. His music doesn't shout or preach — it invites. "Perfect Love" feels less like a sermon and more like a conversation, the kind you'd have sitting on a porch at sunset, talking honestly about the things that keep you up at night. The production is warm and layered, building from intimate verses to a chorus that opens up like arms wide enough to hold everything.

What makes this song resonate beyond church walls is its emotional honesty about fear. Fear of failure, fear of being alone, fear of not measuring up — these aren't exclusively religious concerns. They're human ones. And the song's answer — that there exists a love perfect enough to dissolve those fears — speaks to a longing that lives in everyone, whether they call it God, grace, or simply hope.

## 📖 Lyrics: Key Lines & What They Mean

### "Perfect love casts out all my fears"

**What it means:** A love that is complete, flawless, and unconditional has the power to remove fear entirely — not suppress it, but dissolve it.

**Why it matters:** It's the Biblical foundation of the entire song. "Casts out" means to throw out forcefully — fear isn't gently dismissed, it's expelled.

---

### "You're more than enough"

**What it means:** God's love — and by extension, the love of anyone who truly accepts you — is sufficient. You don't need to earn more, achieve more, or be more.

**Why it matters:** For anyone trapped in the cycle of "I'm not good enough," this line is a direct counter-statement. Enough is enough. You don't need to keep proving yourself.

---

### "When I'm lost, You find me"

**What it means:** Even when he's wandering, confused, or spiritually adrift, God's love pursues and locates him. He doesn't have to find his way back alone.

**Why it matters:** It reframes the relationship between the lost and the divine. You don't find God through effort — God finds you through grace.

---

### "I don't have to be afraid"

**What it means:** A declaration of freedom from fear — not because the scary things have disappeared, but because the love protecting him is stronger than any threat.

**Why it matters:** Fear is acknowledged as real, but it's declared powerless. The song doesn't deny fear exists — it says fear doesn't get the last word.

## 🌍 Cultural & Historical Context

Austin French grew up in Cordele, Georgia — a small town in the American South where faith and music are deeply intertwined. He knew he wanted to be a Christian musician by eighth grade, and after competing on ABC's *Rising Star* (finishing second) and serving as a worship leader in Georgia and Florida, he signed with Fair Trade Services and released his debut album *Wide Open* in 2018.

"Perfect Love" is part of his *Wake Up Sleeper* EP (2020), released during a period when the world was grappling with the COVID-19 pandemic — a time when fear, isolation, and uncertainty were universal experiences. The song's message about perfect love casting out fear felt particularly timely, offering comfort to listeners who were dealing with unprecedented anxiety.

Contemporary Christian music (CCM) has evolved significantly in recent years, with artists like French, Lauren Daigle, and Elevation Worship bridging the gap between worship music and mainstream pop production. "Perfect Love" sits comfortably in this space — musically polished enough for pop playlists, lyrically rooted enough for Sunday services. That dual identity is deliberate: French wants his music to reach people wherever they are, not just inside church walls.

## 🧑‍🎤 About the Artist

Austin French is an American contemporary Christian pop artist from Cordele, Georgia. After a runner-up finish on ABC's *Rising Star*, he devoted his career to worship music, releasing his debut album *Wide Open* (2018) and earning a Dove Award nomination for New Artist of the Year. His music blends pop accessibility with spiritual depth, making worship feel personal and emotionally immediate.
