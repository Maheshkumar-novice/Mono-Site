# 🎵 Waiting for Love — Avicii

> *A euphoric declaration that no matter how many times life knocks you down, love is worth waiting for*

---

## 📀 About the Song

- **Released:** 2015
- **Album:** Stories
- **Genre:** EDM, Progressive House, Dance Pop
- **In one line:** A person walks through the worst days of the week and finds that love — patient, faithful — is waiting at the end.

---

## 🎭 Themes & Emotions

"Waiting for Love" is Avicii at his most hopeful — a song that takes the simplest idea in the world (love is worth waiting for) and turns it into something that feels like sunlight breaking through clouds. The production lifts and soars, the melody is impossible to forget, and the message is as direct as a heartbeat: keep going, because love is coming.

The genius of the song is its structure. It uses the days of the week as a timeline for emotional recovery. Monday is rock bottom — broken, defeated. Tuesday is resignation — he's given up hoping. But by Wednesday, something shifts. He opens his arms again, dares to believe. Thursday, he knows what he's waiting for. Friday, he's grateful. Saturday, he's on fire. It's a compressed narrative of healing that takes the listener from despair to joy in under four minutes.

But this isn't just romantic love. Avicii and the songwriters crafted something bigger — a song about the universal human search for connection and meaning. The lyrics emphasize that we're all fighting the same fight, all looking for the same warmth. It's a song about solidarity disguised as a dance track.

The music video deepens this reading beautifully. It follows an elderly man crossing the countryside on a mobility scooter to find his missing wife, experiencing flashbacks of their life together along the way. It reframes the song's euphoric energy as something quieter and more profound: love isn't just for the young and beautiful. It persists. It endures. It waits.

After Avicii's tragic death in 2018, "Waiting for Love" — like all his music — took on an additional layer of meaning. The optimism in the song feels both more precious and more painful, knowing that the man who created it was fighting his own battles with depression and exhaustion. The song remains, though — patient, hopeful, and waiting.

## 📖 Lyrics: Key Lines & What They Mean

### "Monday left me broken, Tuesday I was through with hoping"

**What it means:** The week starts at its lowest point — emotionally shattered on Monday, completely without hope by Tuesday.

**Why it matters:** Using specific days makes the pain feel real and structured, like a diary entry. Everyone knows what a bad Monday feels like.

---

### "Wednesday my empty arms were open"

**What it means:** By mid-week, despite everything, he opens himself up again — arms spread, ready to receive whatever comes. Vulnerability returns.

**Why it matters:** The shift from closed to open happens without explanation. That's how hope works — it doesn't arrive logically. It just reappears.

---

### "Thursday waiting for love, waiting for love"

**What it means:** Now he knows what he's waiting for. The vague emptiness of Monday has become a specific longing: love.

**Why it matters:** Naming what you need is the first step toward finding it. The repetition turns "waiting for love" into a mantra.

---

### "Where do we begin, the rubble or our sins?"

**What it means:** After everything has fallen apart, where do you start rebuilding? Do you start with the physical damage (the rubble) or the moral damage (the sins)?

**Why it matters:** This is the song's most thoughtful line. It acknowledges that recovery isn't simple — there's wreckage everywhere, and you have to choose where to begin.

---

### "Every night I try my best to dream tomorrow makes it better"

**What it means:** Each night he goes to sleep hoping that the next day will be an improvement — using dreams as a bridge between today's pain and tomorrow's possibility.

**Why it matters:** It captures the daily act of choosing hope. Not a grand decision, but a small nightly prayer that things will improve.

## 🌍 Cultural & Historical Context

Avicii — born Tim Bergling in Stockholm, Sweden, in 1989 — was one of the most influential electronic music producers of the 2010s. By the time "Waiting for Love" was released in 2015, he had already produced global hits like "Levels," "Wake Me Up," and "Hey Brother," bridging the gap between EDM festivals and mainstream pop radio in a way nobody had done before.

"Waiting for Love" was the lead single from *Stories*, his second and final studio album. The song was co-written with Martin Garrix and featured vocals by Simon Aldred of the band Cherry Ghost, though Aldred was uncredited on the original release. It premiered at Ultra Music Festival 2015 and quickly became one of the biggest dance tracks of the year.

Avicii's death by suicide in April 2018 at age 28 cast his entire catalogue in a different light. Songs about hope, persistence, and finding meaning — which once felt like festival anthems — became something closer to prayers. "Waiting for Love" is particularly poignant in this context: a song about enduring bad days and believing that love will come, created by someone who was privately struggling with the very despair the song describes. His family later revealed he had been battling mental health issues and the physical toll of relentless touring. The Tim Bergling Foundation was established in his memory to support mental health awareness.

## 🎯 Fun Facts

- The song was co-produced with Martin Garrix, who was just 18 at the time — two generations of EDM meeting on one track.
- The music video follows an elderly man on a mobility scooter journeying to find his wife — a deliberate contrast to the typical EDM video featuring young clubgoers.
- Simon Aldred of Cherry Ghost provided the vocals but was uncredited on the original release. His warm, non-electronic voice was key to giving the song its emotional weight.

---

## 🧑‍🎤 About the Artist

Avicii was the stage name of Tim Bergling (1989–2018), a Swedish DJ, producer, and songwriter who became one of the most successful electronic musicians in history. From his bedroom in Stockholm to headlining the world's biggest festivals, he pioneered a style that fused EDM with folk, country, and pop influences. His death at 28 was a devastating loss to music, but his catalogue — hopeful, euphoric, deeply human — continues to reach new listeners every day.

## 💬 Why This Song Is Worth Your Time

"Waiting for Love" teaches you how English structures time and emotion — using days of the week as emotional landmarks, phrases like "through with hoping" and "open arms" that appear constantly in conversation. But the song's deepest gift is its insistence that bad days end. Monday's brokenness doesn't last forever. By Thursday, you remember what you're living for. For anyone learning English in a new country, far from home, navigating hard days — this song is a reminder that love, in all its forms, is patient. It waits.
